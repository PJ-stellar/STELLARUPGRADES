Monthly payments depend on system size and term length. No. Financeit offers $0 down financing. CEIP also requires no upfront payment. The mortgage roll-in option adds the cost to your existing mortgage at renewal with no additional down payment. Only the cash purchase option requires payment upfront, in exchange for a 10% discount on total system cost.
CEIP stands for Clean Energy Improvement Program. It allows homeowners in participating Alberta municipalities — including Edmonton, Leduc, and Spruce Grove — to finance solar through their property tax bill. No credit check is required, and the financing transfers automatically to the new owner if you sell your home. Contact us or your municipality for current rates and terms.
Yes. Financeit has no prepayment penalties. You can pay off the balance at any time without extra fees. Many homeowners choose to make lump-sum payments when they have extra funds, reducing their total interest cost. CEIP early payoff terms vary by municipality.
Yes. Studies consistently show that solar panels increase home resale value. A solar system that eliminates a $200/month electricity bill adds significant value to the property because the buyer inherits $0 electricity costs. This is especially true in Alberta's deregulated market where electricity rates continue to rise. The increase in home value often exceeds the remaining loan balance, making solar a net-positive investment even if you sell before the loan is paid off.
With Financeit or mortgage roll-in, you would pay off the remaining balance at closing — similar to any other loan. Since solar typically increases home value by more than the remaining balance, this is usually a net gain. With CEIP, the financing transfers automatically to the new owner as part of the property tax, so there is nothing to pay off at sale.
